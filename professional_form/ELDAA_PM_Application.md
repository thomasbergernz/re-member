| 1. **Effective Communication Skills** – Practice deep listening, open-ended questioning, and holding space for emotions without judgment. |  |
| 1. **Advocacy & Empowerment** – Support clients in navigating medical and healthcare systems, ensuring their voices are heard. |  |
| 1. **Understanding of Cultural & Spiritual Diversity** – Respect and support diverse traditions, religious beliefs, and cultural practices around death and dying. |  |
| 1. **Shows initiative** - The ability to anticipate what a persons needs might be and prepare accordingly. Know how to seek information for what you don’t know. |  |
| 1. **Compassionate Presence** – Leave your problems/issues/thoughts/beliefs at the door. Bring a quiet, calm presence always. Follow the lead of the person and their whanau. |  |
| 1. **Ongoing Education & Development** – Commit to continuous learning in end-of-life care, including staying informed about legal and ethical considerations. |  |
| 1. **Self-Care & Professional Boundaries** – Maintain personal well-being and set healthy emotional boundaries to sustain this work long-term. Know when to step back if your own issues will affect your mahi. |  |
| 1. **Knowledge of End-of-Life Options** – Be well-versed in palliative care, hospice, voluntary assisted dying, and home death options. |  |
| 1. **Business acumen** – Provide a contractual agreement for services up front with Clients, be willing to offer a sliding scale of fees, and invoice in a timely manner. |  |
| 1. **Networking & Referrals** – Connect families with additional support, such as grief counselors, deathcare professionals, or community resources. |  |
| 1. **Holistic support** - **emotional, spiritual, cultural, practical and social -** during an illness journey towards recovery or death, while helping to the person to live their best life. |  |
| 1. **Illness journey advocacy and empowerment** - Coaching and supporting clients to navigate medical and healthcare systems, physically attending specialist visits where helpful. |  |
| 1. **Legacy & Life Review** – Facilitate storytelling, memory-sharing, letter-writing, or other legacy projects to honour the dying person's life. |  |
| 1. **Holistic Advance Care Planning** – Work with families to create a care plan that aligns with the dying person’s values, beliefs, and preferences. Assist clients in discussing and documenting their wishes, including advance directives. |  |
| 1. **Vigil Planning & Support** – Help plan the atmosphere, rituals, and presence for the final days and hours, ensuring a peaceful environment. |  |
| 1. **Practical Assistance during illness -** Be willing to assist with personal cares, and practical tasks if required. |  |
| 1. **Funeral & Memorial Planning** – Help families explore meaningful ways to honour their loved one, including alternative and eco-friendly funeral options. |  |
| 1. **Body care and After Death care** – Be knowledgeable and comfortable assisting families with after-death care, including washing, dressing, and cooling the body. |  |
| 1. **Grief & Bereavement Awareness** – Offer compassionate support and connect to resources for grief, understanding different ways people process loss. |  |
| 1. **Interdisciplinary Collaboration** – Working alongside medical, hospice, iwi/hapū, and community professionals. |  |
